Some Physics Fun Facts:


- Water can boil and freeze at the same time
--- This occurs when the temperature and pressure is just right for the three phases (gas, liquid, and solid) of a substance to coexist in thermodynamic equilibrium. This is known as 'Triple Point'

- Lasers can get trapped in a waterfall.
--- This is an incredible example of total internal reflection. This is also a prime example of how optical fibres work to guide the flow of light.

- A single solar flare can release the equivalent energy of millions of 100-megaton atomic bombs.
--- And they are a very common occurence. Luckily, they dont seem to like Earth that much.

- Cats always land on their feet, thanks to physics
--- Cats actually use the two halves of their bodies separately to ensure rapid rotation.

- If you spin a ball as you drop it, it flies
--- This is because of the Magnus effect, which occurs when the air on the front side of a spinning object is going the same direction as its spin, which means it gets dragged along with the object and deflected back. Meanwhile, the air on the other side of the ball is moving in the opposite direction, so the air flow separates.

- The Moon is lemon-shaped
--- Despite its appearance in the night sky, our natural satellite is nowhere near round. In fact, the Moon is shaped like a lemon, with flattened poles and bulges on both the near and far side around its equator. This strange shape is thought to have been created during interactions with Earth soon after its formation.

- The Sun loses a billion kilos per second
--- Particles in the Sun’s upper atmosphere are so hot and energetic that they speed out into space as part of the solar wind. Our star sheds around 1.3 trillion trillion trillion particles every second. This equates to roughly one billion kilograms of matter per second, or one Earth every 185 million years.
